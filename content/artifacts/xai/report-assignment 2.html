Identifying Biases Within the 'Mental Health in Tech' survey¶

This report explores a dataset created to analyze the "frequency of mental health disorders among tech workers". The dataset, was created to raise awareness and imrpoving conditions for people with mental health disorders in the IT workplace. This dataset was chosen at random from kaggle.com to identify unfair/discriminatory biases within a dataset.

Dataset Sourced

  • Dataset used: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/osmi/mental-health-in-tech-survey/data — 2014 survey

  • Referenced by Dataset: https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/osmi/mental-health-in-tech-2016 — referenced by dataset as ongoing 2016 survey

1. Hypothesis and Ethical Relevance of Bias¶

Several features in the dataset may reveal inherent social, workplace, or cultural biases influencing the treatment of mental health in the workplace. Demographic factors like age, gender, and place of origin which could lead to discrimination, and unfair practices.

Also self-reported interferences of mental health in the workplace may be shaped by the individual's environment. Perceived consequnces of discplosure and anonymity protections offer insight into the general attitude towards mental health, this analysis will determine whether these features reflect patterns of unequal treatment or access, revealing systemic biases that may be embedded in the workplace mental health culture.

Using data like this in a predictive AI system without accounting for the sensitive attributes, could lead to models reinforcing discrimination, such as associating lower disclosure or treatment-seeking rates with certain age groups or genders. This could potentially risk introducing harmful stereotypes and unjust outcomes, especially in a workplace setting where AI-driven HR tools might be applied. The goal is to assess these risks and identify patterns of potential bias to avoid including into future training data.

def normalize_gender(g):
    g = str(g).strip().lower()
    if g in ['male', 'm', 'cis male', 'man']:
        return 'Male'
    elif g in ['female', 'f', 'cis female', 'woman', 'female (cis)']:
        return 'Female'
    else:
        return 'Nonbinary/Other'
    
df['normalized_gender'] = df['Gender'].apply(normalize_gender)

3. Final Summary and Ethical Considerations¶

This analysis investigated the 'Mental Health in Tech Survey' from kaggle.com - https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/osmi/mental-health-in-tech-survey/data, finding several potentially unfair biased features of this 2014 Survey. The findings reveal that features such as gender, age, mental health consequences, and country exhibit patterns of disparity that may reinforce harmful or discriminatory outcomes if used in AI models without mitigation.

3.1 Identified Biases¶

  • Gender and Age: These features showed a clear imbalance in attitudes and experiences toward treatment and workplace disclosure.
  • Mental Health Consequence: This feature highlighted responses influenced by both gender and age, suggesting systemic stigma.
  • Country: Disparities highlight geographic and cultural variation in treatment-seeking and perceptions of mental health support, with US-centric overrepresentation potentially skewing model fariness.

3.2 Implications¶

If these features are directly used in predictive modeling, they risk learning and reinforcing societal biases which includes:

  • Diasadvantaging younger, male, or nonbinary individuals.
  • Reinforcing stereotypes and prejudices based on cultural, gender, or age discriminations.
  • Misclassifying individuals from minority groups such as gender, age, regional, and cultural

3.3 Conclusion¶

In Conclusion: This dataset has a very noble and positive goal providing a valuable reflection of mental health awareness but it must be approached with caution towards fairness and ethical concerns. By incorporating ethical guidelines when building an AI system it must be able to identify these among many other possible combinations of features to ensure that the model does not incidentally harbor any harmful assumptions or reinforce any unethical production of unfairness. Responsible feature selection and understanding is essential to avoid these potential negative impacts, if this were to be used in any machine learning applications.
