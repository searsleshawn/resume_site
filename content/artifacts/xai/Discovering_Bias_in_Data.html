Overview¶

This investigation examines statistical bias within the Mental Health in Tech Survey dataset through exploratory data analysis and fairness metrics. The goal was to determine whether demographic and workplace attributes could introduce bias into machine learning predictions and to identify features that may require careful consideration in responsible AI development.

To support accurate statistical interpretation, the analysis environment was configured to preserve full output visibility, ensuring fairness metrics and subgroup distributions could be examined without truncation.

    if re.search(r'\bfem(ale)?\b', val):
        return 'Female'
    if re.search(r'\bmale\b', val):

    return None

Data bias detection

Definition:

  • Protected group is a feature $\mathbf{S}$ that belongs to $\mathbf{X}$ having $\mathbf{N}$ data points and ${D}$ dimensions
  • Advantegous group / Privileged is a level $\mathbf{T}_{a}$ of the feature $\mathbf{S}$ having $\mathbf{N}_{a}$ data points belonging to $\mathbf{X}$ having a higher probability of favourable outcome and is also called $\mathbf{S}=1$. Also $\mathbf{S_a}$
  • Disadvantegous group / Unprivileged is a level $\mathbf{T}_{d}$ of the feature $\mathbf{S}$ having $\mathbf{N}_{d}$ data points belonging to X which has a higher probability of favourable outcome and is also called $\mathbf{S}=0$. Also $\mathbf{S_d}$
  • Actual lables / target with data point $\mathbf{N}$ are represnted by $\mathbf{Y}$ having values ${y}$
  • Predicted labels are represented $\hat{Y}$

Statistical Parity Difference and Disparate Impact¶

Probability of all groups under protected class to have same probability of favourable outcome
Fairness Through Awareness

Statistical Parity Difference: $$ \begin{align} \mathbb{P}({Y} = 1 {|} \mathbf{S} = \mathbf{T}_{a}) = \mathbb{P}( {Y}=1{|}\mathbf{S} = \mathbf{T}_{d}) \end{align} $$

Disparate Impact:

$$ \begin{align} \frac{\mathbb{P}(Y=1{|}S=T_a)} {\mathbb{P}(Y=1{|}S=T_a)} \end{align} $$

3. Results Interpretation: What Do the Bias Metrics Mean?¶

The calculated statistical parity difference and disparate impact for each protected attribute are summarized below:

  • Gender and Age Group: Show the largest disparities, indicating that males and certain age groups may be more or less likely to seek treatment.
  • Employment Type: Features such as working remotely, working in tech, or being self-employed also show disparities, suggesting that workplace context influences access or willingness to seek help.
  • Benefits: Employees who have access to mental health benefits tend to report higher rates of seeking treatment, reflecting the importance of workplace support.
  • Country: There are observable differences between countries, likely due to cultural, systemic, or resource-driven factors.

Interpretation:
If the statistical parity difference exceeds 0.1, or disparate impact falls outside [0.9, 1.1], this signals a potential unfairness. This highlights where disparities are strongest and suggest areas for further investigation or intervention to ensure a model's fairness.

5. Interpretation & Implications for Responsible AI¶

Our bias analysis reveals significant disparities in mental health treatment outcomes based on protected attributes such as gender, age group, and employment context.

  • Groups with higher statistical parity difference or disparate impact are systematically more or less likely to seek treatment, which may reflect underlying societal, structural, or workplace barriers.
  • Potential causes include cultural stigma, lack of resources, or uneven support across organizations and countries.

Implications:

  • For practitioners: Predictive models trained on this data may inherit or amplify these biases, leading to unfair recommendations or interventions for underrepresented groups.
  • For organizations: There is a need for targeted outreach and policy changes to ensure equitable access to mental health resources.
  • For data scientists: Regular fairness audits are crucial to identify and mitigate hidden biases before deploying machine learning solutions.

These results emphasize the importance of responsible AI practices and ongoing fairness monitoring in real-world applications, especially in sensitive domains like mental health.
